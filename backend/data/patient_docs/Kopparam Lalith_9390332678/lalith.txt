MEDICAL CONSULTATION REPORT

Chief Complaint:
Patient reports persistent stomach pain for the past 5 days. The pain is described as a burning sensation in the upper abdomen and becomes worse after consuming spicy or oily foods. The patient also reports occasional nausea and bloating.

History of Present Illness:

* Stomach pain began approximately 5 days ago.
* Pain severity ranges from 5 to 7 on a scale of 10.
* Symptoms are worse after meals.
* Occasional nausea reported.
* No vomiting.
* No blood in stool.
* No recent travel history.
* No recent hospitalization.

Past Medical History:

* Gastritis diagnosed 2 years ago.
* Acid reflux (GERD).
* No history of gastrointestinal surgery.

Current Medications:

* Antacid tablets as needed.
* Proton Pump Inhibitor (PPI) once daily.

Allergies:

* No known drug allergies.

Vital Signs:
Blood Pressure: 124/82 mmHg
Heart Rate: 78 bpm
Respiratory Rate: 16 breaths/min
Temperature: 98.4°F
Oxygen Saturation: 99%

Physical Examination:

* Mild tenderness in the upper abdominal region.
* No abdominal swelling observed.
* No signs of dehydration.
* Bowel sounds normal.
* Patient alert and oriented.

Laboratory Findings:
Complete Blood Count (CBC): Within normal limits.
Liver Function Test (LFT): Normal.
Kidney Function Test (KFT): Normal.

Assessment:
Findings are suggestive of gastritis with possible acid reflux exacerbation. No immediate signs of gastrointestinal bleeding or acute abdominal emergency were identified during examination.

Diagnosis:

1. Gastritis
2. Gastroesophageal Reflux Disease (GERD)

Treatment Plan:

* Continue prescribed PPI medication.
* Avoid spicy, oily, and acidic foods.
* Increase water intake.
* Eat smaller and more frequent meals.
* Avoid lying down immediately after eating.
* Follow-up review in 7 days.

Emergency Warning Signs:
Seek immediate medical attention if any of the following occur:

* Vomiting blood
* Black or tarry stools
* Severe sudden abdominal pain
* Persistent vomiting
* Difficulty breathing
* Loss of consciousness

Follow-Up Notes:
Patient advised to maintain a food diary to identify dietary triggers. Symptoms should be monitored for worsening intensity or frequency.
